Time passes through us like wind through a corridor.
<br />Some moments carry a particular scent or light, lingering briefly before they fade.
<br />Some feelings cannot be held by a lens; some memories resist being collected in images.
<br />Perhaps keeping a written backup of a moment's feeling is the gentlest, yet most effective, way to resist forgetting.

## 2025

### The new place

On moving night we set the little lamp on the floor, boxes still piled everywhere. We used a cardboard box as a table and ate takeout. It only felt like we'd truly moved in when we woke up the next day.

### A short trip to a small town

On a whim we headed to a seaside town. Arrived at dawn and had hot soy milk by the station. Walked a lap along the seawall, the wind knocking my hat every which way. Tucked the train ticket into my wallet on the way back.

### Coming home at night

In the evening I reached the corner I always walk past. The shop's name had changed twice. The rain had just stopped, the pavement glossy with reflections. I stood a while before walking on.

### The old bookstore

Caught in the rain, I ducked into the old bookstore, the little bell at the door jingling nonstop. Picked out a secondhand essay collection and read a few pages right there. The owner wrapped the book in old newspaper.

### Dinner by candlelight

Once the power went out, so we ate by candlelight. The food was simple, but the room was so quiet you could hear the rain. Afterward we restocked the backup candles.

### A two-photo frame

Sorting through the album, I picked two old photos and slipped them into the same frame. Set it by the window where the morning light falls just right. I glance at it every time I pass.

### Reunion after years

A friend I hadn't seen in years came to visit. We walked down to the river and talked late into the night, still with so much left unsaid. Before parting we promised not to let so much time pass again.

### Night runs

As it cooled down I started night running, the same long street every time. Streetlamps passed one by one, my footsteps clear and steady. I slept soundly after getting home.

> No great events this year, but plenty of moments worth looking back on.

## 2024

### By the river at dusk

At dusk I went to sit by the river for a while; someone was playing music in the distance. The wind was light, and fine shards of light trembled on the water. I stayed until dark before heading back.

### A handwritten letter

I wrote a letter to a faraway friend and used up two sheets. Only at the end did I realize how much I never say out loud day to day. I read it once more before sending it.

### A small-town getaway

Over the weekend we visited a nearby small town and had the local breakfast. Brought back a few postcards and pinned them by the desk. A short trip, but a comfortable one.

## 2023

### First snow

The first snow fell so lightly, the streets so quiet. I stood at the window and watched for a long time.


### The book corner

I repainted the old bookshelf and arranged the books I reread often, deliberately leaving one shelf empty. At night I switch on a small lamp and sit a while.

### Seeing an old friend again

Old friends gathered for a meal. We had all changed a little, but the laughter was still familiar. When we parted, I felt warm inside.
